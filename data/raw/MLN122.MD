|           Description: | Course introduction: Political economics of Marxism – Leninism is an economic theory and scientific discipline on political economy developed by C. Marx, Engels and later Lenin in a new period, focusing on the capitalist mode of production and the production and economic exchange relations consistent with the capitalist mode of production, thereby clarifying the nature and phenomena of economic processes, laying a foundation to solve matters related to theories of Marxism - Leninism. The core of the Marxist-Leninist political economy is the surplus value theory of Marx.  Course objectives: + Provide core knowledge of Marxist–Leninist Political Economy, ensuring fundamental, systematic, and scientific foundations, connected with practical realities and updated knowledge. Ensure basic, systematic, scientific qualities, updating new knowledge, linked to reality, creativity, skills, thinking, and learner qualities. Improve continuity, overcome duplication, increase integration and reduce load, removing content that is no longer appropriate or purely academic for students at non-theoretical vocational schools and universities. + Develop analytical and evaluative thinking, identifying the essence of economic interest relations in socio-economic development, linked with social responsibility and future career positions. + Strengthen the standpoint and ideological consciousness of Marxism–Leninism among students. + Foster critical thinking, reasoning competence, and practical application, particularly in the context of industrialization, modernization, scientific and technological advancement, and the development of the private sector within the socialist-oriented market economy. Emphasize civic roles and social responsibility. Content of the course: Political economics of Marxism – Leninism focuses on investigating economic relations in capitalist society and in-depth studies on laws of this economy, namely: + Mention about commodities, commodity production and economic laws of commodity production (in capitalism) + Concentrate on analyzing basic economic laws of capitalism with the main focus is surplus value production + Analysis of the movement of specific capitalism and social capital reproduction + Consider forms of capitalism and forms of surplus value + Study monopoly capitalism and State monopoly capitalism + Research on the socialist-oriented market economy in Vietnam + Analysis of Vietnam’s process of industrialization, modernization, and international economic integration in the context of the contemporary era Teaching methods: Lecturing combined with slide presentations, and applying AI to support the learning process. Providing instructions and assigning group projects. Organizing and guiding students in group discussions, presentations, and the creation of creative products. |
