|           Description: | I. Orientation requirements for course implementation: The course provides basic knowledge and skills of Japanese at elementary level 1 (equivalent to A1 level) for students learning Japanese as a second foreign language at University. II. Course Objectives: After completing the course, students can achieve the following goals: 1) Proficiency in writing and reading the Japanese alphabet (Hiragana, Katakana) 2) Master about 200 basic vocabulary words, the writing and reading of more than 30 Kanji characters, and about 20 basic grammar structures used in elementary conversation. 3) Understand and use familiar expressions used in daily life to meet specific communication needs such as self-introduction (name, nationality, occupation, hobbies, etc.); asking for locations, communicating with staff to ask for prices, ordering food, etc.; telling about daily activities, asking about the working hours of organizations. 4) Practice teamwork skills and self-development through group activities in class (role play...) and extracurricular activities. |
